# Clinical Volume Safety and Administration Protocol
**Protocol ID:** Nasal-Irrigation-Protocol
**Administration Status:** Under strict professional supervision only. **NEVER FOR HOME DISPENSING.**

---

## 1. Regulatory Safety Statement
*   **Localized Application:** This protocol utilizes a localized, volume-based mechanical irrigation model. The in-clinic application consists of automated metered sprays per naris, or a specific volume of micro-filtered solution per irrigation cycle, as determined by the attending physician's assessment of mucosal pathology.
*   **Infant/Pediatric Restriction:** **This protocol is not intended for infants or pediatric patients.**
*   **No Biometric Calculations:** Application is volume-limited based on localized mucosal tissue tolerance and is not calculated using biometric or weight-based ($mg/kg$) formulas.

---

## 2. Clinical Justification for High-Volume Application
When clinical providers utilize high-volume washes or repeated application cycles during an in-office procedure, the clinical justification is documented as follows:

[High-Volume Mucosal Delivery]\
│\
▼ (Mechanical Lavage & Biofilm Disruption)\
[Dilution and Physical Flushing of Mucus Matrices]\
│\
▼ (Direct Saturation of Target Areas)\
[Topical Application without Systemic Absorption]


1.  **Mechanical Lavage vs. Systemic Absorption:** High-volume intranasal application does not increase systemic concentrations. Because the substances (e.g., Capsaicin, Hydrogen Peroxide, Hypochlorous Acid) function via local, topical contact, high volumes are used to mechanically flush mucus, disrupt biofilms, and wash away cellular debris.
2.  **Saturation of Target Areas:** Deep sinus cavities may require substantial volume saturation. A high-volume wash ensures that the agents physically reach the anatomical recesses.
3.  **Low Systemic Bioavailability:** Because these compounds break down rapidly upon contact with organic tissue or possess low mucosal absorption, clinical volumes are introduced locally to act on target areas without causing systemic toxicity or overloading hepatic clearance pathways.

---

## 3. Mandatory Regulatory Disclaimer
> **REGULATORY COMPLIANCE NOTICE:** The in-clinic use of these washes is intended for structural, mechanical, and functional clearing of the nasal passages. This protocol and associated documentation are not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent any specific disease, and must not be personalized outside of a licensed physician-patient relationship.

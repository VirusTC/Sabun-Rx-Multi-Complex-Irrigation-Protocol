## 1. Core Defensive Posture: Truth in Sourcing & Application
When a commercial payer or third-party auditor initiates a post-payment audit or reviews a prior-authorization claim for **Sabun Rx**, the billing department must immediately establish the absolute truth of our clinical chemistry, math, and operations:
1. **Zero Misrepresentation:** The clinic explicitly rejects deceptive billing mechanisms. Sabun Rx is never billed using a National Drug Code (NDC) meant for synthetic pharmaceuticals. It is billed honestly as an in-office specialized provider supply (**CPT Code 99070** or unlisted biological supply codes **J3590 / A4649**).
2. **Value-Based Financial Defence:** Auditors flag high-value line items. The clinical defense rests on value-based economics: a specialized, highly effective in-office wash completely eliminates the necessity for a **Functional Endoscopic Sinus Surgery (FESS - CPT 31255)**, which costs insurers an average of **$15,000 to $30,000** per operation.

## 2. Standardized Audit Scripts & Phone Manifests

### Script A: Addressing "Experimental / Investigational" Denials
> **Underwriter Objection:** *"This compound is categorized as an unapproved botanical or over-the-counter dietary supplement and is excluded from coverage."*
>
> **Clinical Response Script:** *"The exclusion you are citing applies strictly to Durable Medical Equipment (DME) or take-home prescriptions meant for self-administration. Sabun Rx is an institutional, professional-grade clinical material applied exclusively by a licensed provider within a controlled procedural environment. It is never dispensed for home use. The FDA has a long-standing precedent recognizing the efficacy of green tea catechins and topical oxidants in treating epithelial lesions. This protocol represents the licensed physician's absolute right to determine the choice of localized mechanical cleansing agents to prevent a costlier $30,000 surgical intervention."*

### Script B: Defending the High Cost of the Specialized Supply (CPT 99070)
> **Auditor Objection:** *"Why does the billing code 99070 for this supply reflect a cost of thousands of dollars when the raw materials are commercially sourced?"*
>
> **Clinical Response Script:** *"The insurance carrier is not being billed a retail markup for a consumer item. The line item explicitly covers the intensive pharmacy compounding, molecular stabilization, and stoichiometric validation required to render the raw ingredients bioavailable and non-toxic for deep sinus recesses. Without the clinician's diagnostic staging, the custom blending ratios, and the controlled delivery infrastructure, the material cannot achieve localized tissue resolution. The payout represents the specialized medical solution and clinical manufacturing, which is fully justified by the patient's objective, documented reduction in mucosal lesions."*

## 3. Objective Medical Record Checklist for Auditors
To ensure a claim is entirely bulletproof against a retrospective audit, the patient's electronic health record (EHR) must contain the following physical evidence:
* [ ] **Diagnostic Baseline Imaging:** Endoscopic tracking media documenting severe mucosal sores, hyperkeratosis, or active exogenous vector presence.
* [ ] **Signed Patient Informed Consent:** A fully executed copy of the regulatory disclosure form proving the patient accepted the structural/functional protocol.
* [ ] **Post-Procedure Evacuation Log:** Documented evidence of the clinical vacuum suction loop used to evacuate the lysed materials and spent solution from the sinus pathways.
* [ ] **Post-Treatment Outcome Media:** Photographic or clinical evidence showing the physical resolution of the internal tissue defects.
